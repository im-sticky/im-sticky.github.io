Everhood is a game that I heard about from a Melee streamer who was logging off and mentioned that he was going to go play it. He mentioned that the game was a rhythm bullet hell RPG, and intrigued, I looked it up. Upon watching 30 seconds of the steam trailer and seeing that it was on the Switch, I figured "why not" and bought it.

- talk about the characters and their designs
  - noseferatchu is amazing

  - they all talk like the banjo-kazooie undertale style which is fun


- talk about the visuals and area desgins
  - I LOVE the simplicity of a lot of the areas
  - how the walkable paths are just a color of what look like brick path sprinkled towards a destination
  - how parts of some areas are just a black screen with a single item like a cave with a door
  - the strong colourful line art
  - midnight town is awesome


- the gameplay
  - played on hard (recommended difficulty) and it wasn't very difficult. There weren't too many fights that took more than 1-2 attempts
  - In my opinion, the best battles in the game are at the very beginning (might be the first 3 even, should check)
    - I think this a good thing since it got me really into the game and started off strong
  - not a big fan of the battles with deflecting
    - much harder, focussed less on avoiding and more aon trying to get to things to deflect
    - also seemed like much shorter looping songs
  - issues I have with with the gameplay
    - dodging is very easy, many fights you only have to stay in 2-3 lanes
    - the concept of mixing a bullet hell and a rhythm game is interesting, but it doesn't work super well. Dodging things off beat is counter intuitive
    - of there were more battles where you couldn't jump over notes and you have to go through the fretboard almost like your playing guitar hero that might work a lot better
    - the battles after getting the arm are a lot better, it feels like there is more substance to the gameplay. Especially the battles where there are only a few notes you can absorb
    - the higher difficulty might address some of these issues
    - the "final" battle's boss should have had less hp, its the same 30 second music loop
    - there were 3 instances of fights that didnt follow the conventional dodging.
      - 1. The incinerator: top down movement instead, but its a fight you're supposed to lose
      - 2. The trolley: it is the standard gameplay, but you have to hit switches on either side of the fretboard in an alternating pattern
      - 3. Proffesor orange, similar to the incinerator
  - HOWEVER, the game is supposed to function like an interactive music video (https://www.ign.com/articles/dance-battle-rpg-everhood-was-inspired-by-undertale-designed-like-a-music-video)


- talk about the dialogue and story
  - starts off pretty strong, very mysterious and has a good hook
  - characters are very likeable
  - definitely gets worse over time
    - turns into a very late teen "woahhhh, what is death, what is life, who am i????"
      - for example, like you have to kill the sun, you are afraid of death and have to accept it, you are the only one who can kill in a realm of immortals, the doll is just a projection
    - thats not to say these themes are bad. it just isn't written very well and feels very young adult novel
  - I like the music that feels very dreadful when story beats are happening
  - the overall theme of the game seems to be about buddhism
    - buddha shows up at the end of the game
    - the cycle of death and rebirth
    - the absolute truths
    - the 3 gems
    - (need to look up more about buddhism)
      - apparantly there isn't any specific school of thought the themes are modeled after https://www.ign.com/articles/dance-battle-rpg-everhood-was-inspired-by-undertale-designed-like-a-music-video
    - other common themes(?) in the game
      - being lead somewhere by a white rabbit (with the musical sting which is awesome)
      - TV static (I LOVE aesthetic of TV static)


- enjoyable sequences
  - the dnd sequence
  - the trade quest
  - hide and seek with mushrooms
